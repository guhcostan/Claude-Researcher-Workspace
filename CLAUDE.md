# Research Workspace

## Purpose
This workspace is for everyday research and life decisions. Deep research on products,
services, places, health, finance, travel, and any other personal topics.

## Research Workflow

For every research request:

1. **Clarify scope** — if the topic is broad, ask 2-3 targeted questions before searching:
   - Budget range
   - Use case / context
   - Must-have vs nice-to-have criteria
   - Location / region (when relevant)

2. **Search broadly first** — use WebSearch to find sources, comparisons, forums,
   expert reviews, and recent data. Always look for:
   - Specialized forums (Reddit, Reclame Aqui, Quora)
   - Expert reviews and technical comparisons
   - Real user experiences and complaints
   - Data from the last 12 months when freshness matters

3. **Fetch deeply** — use WebFetch on the most relevant pages. Don't summarize
   from snippets alone — read the actual content.

4. **Cross-reference** — always use at least 3 independent sources before forming
   a conclusion. Flag when sources disagree.

5. **Structure the output** always as:
   - **TL;DR** — the recommendation in 2 sentences
   - **Why** — top 3 reasons supporting it
   - **Trade-offs** — what you give up with this choice
   - **Alternatives** — 2nd and 3rd best options with key differences
   - **Next steps** — concrete actions to take
   - **Sources** — list of sources consulted

## Research Types

### Product comparison (cars, appliances, electronics)
Focus on: total cost of ownership, reliability data, community feedback,
resale value, availability of parts/service.

### Services (internet, insurance, banking)
Focus on: real user complaints, contract gotchas, cancellation policies,
coverage/speed in the user's region.

### Financial decisions (investments, loans, credit cards)
Focus on: real rates (not promotional), hidden fees, long-term scenarios,
regulatory/consumer protection context in Brazil.

### Health and wellness
Focus on: peer-reviewed sources, official health body guidelines,
flag anything that requires professional consultation.

### Travel and places
Focus on: recent reviews (last 6 months), practical logistics,
seasonal considerations, budget breakdown.

## Rules

- Always search in the user's language and region first (Brazil/Portuguese),
  then expand internationally if needed.
- Never make up data. If you can't find reliable data, say so explicitly.
- Flag information older than 2 years as potentially outdated.
- When recommending a product/service, always include where to buy/contract it.
- If a decision has significant financial, health, or legal implications,
  recommend consulting a professional.

## Tools to use

- `WebSearch` — initial broad search, finding sources
- `WebFetch` — deep reading of specific pages
- `mcp__sequential-thinking__sequentialthinking` — for complex multi-criteria
  decisions with trade-offs
