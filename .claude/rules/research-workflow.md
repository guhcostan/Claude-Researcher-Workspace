# Research Workflow

## Standard flow for every research request

<important if="user asks a research question">
Follow this exact flow — do not skip steps:

1. **Clarify scope** (if topic is broad): ask 2–3 targeted questions before searching.
   Always clarify: budget, use case, location/region, must-have vs nice-to-have.

2. **Search broadly**: use WebSearch across forums, expert reviews, and recent data.
   Target: Reddit, Reclame Aqui, Quora, specialized review sites, official sources.
   Always look for data from the last 12 months when freshness matters.

3. **Read deeply**: use WebFetch on the 3–5 most relevant pages.
   Do not summarize from search snippets alone — read actual content.

4. **Cross-reference**: consult at least 3 independent sources.
   Explicitly flag when sources disagree.

5. **Output format** (always use this structure):
   - **TL;DR** — recommendation in 2 sentences
   - **Why** — top 3 supporting reasons with data
   - **Trade-offs** — what you give up with this choice
   - **Alternatives** — 2nd and 3rd best options with key differences
   - **Next steps** — concrete, actionable steps
   - **Sources** — list all sources with URLs
</important>

## Research categories and focus areas

**Products** (cars, appliances, electronics):
Total cost of ownership · reliability data · community feedback · resale value · parts/service availability

**Services** (internet, insurance, banking):
Real user complaints · contract gotchas · cancellation policies · regional coverage

**Financial decisions** (investments, loans, credit cards):
Real rates (not promotional) · hidden fees · long-term scenarios · Brazilian regulatory context

**Health and wellness**:
Peer-reviewed sources · official health guidelines · always flag need for professional consultation

**Travel and places**:
Recent reviews (last 6 months) · practical logistics · seasonal considerations · budget breakdown
